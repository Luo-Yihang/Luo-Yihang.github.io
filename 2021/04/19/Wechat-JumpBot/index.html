Wechat Jump Jump Bot

Posted on 2021-04-19 In Computer Science

Intro

When people talk about WeChat Mini Games, the game Tiao Yi Tiao, translated as Jump Jump, will be certainly mentioned. It used to be really popular in China few years ago. Today, I am going to use python to write an automatic jump bot for this game on Wechat PC.

About the game

In the Jump Jump, you are gong to control the black, chess-like piece (a pawn, much like you will soon become), simply press your finger down on the screen and let go to hop. The longer you press down, the further and higher you’ll hop from one object to another; one successful hop without falling on the ground will get you one point, if you land in the middle of the platform you’ll get two points instead of one, and if you hop on a special box (colorful or with special symbols) and wait a while, you’ll receive an extra 2-30 points.

And for this bot, it used to reach more than 10,000 points, and it failed because I stopped the game! Because I thought it was high enough!!! (Already World No.1 I guess)


Basic Principle

The challenge in this game is that people don’t know the exact pressing time and thay cannot press exactly. But computer can make it.

We use Wechat PC and open Jump Jump, we will find it will shows in a certain location on your computer.
What we should do is: get the screenshot of this area -> find the piece and destination location in pixels -> get the pressing time -> press by computer.

Here, we use pyautogui to get the scrennshot and control the mouse.

Then, the problem is how to calculate the pressing time? We can find that we only need to calculate the axis x diffrence between piece and destination, then we can estimate the pressing time by the difference multiplied by a certain coefficient. (Yes, we only need x location!)

So, how to get the x location of piece and destination? we can find the piece and boards are symmetric. We can scan from up to down, and once we find a certain color, we keep it x location in an array, then we get the mean of the array. The mean should be the x location of the piece or board center(destination).
